TOPIC: Probability Distributions

BINOMIAL DISTRIBUTION B(n, p):
- P(X = k) = C(n,k) × pᵏ × (1-p)^(n-k)
- Mean: μ = np
- Variance: σ² = np(1-p)
- Use when: fixed n trials, each success/fail, constant p, independent

POISSON DISTRIBUTION:
- P(X = k) = (λᵏ × e^(-λ)) / k!
- Mean = Variance = λ
- Use when: counting rare events in fixed interval

NORMAL DISTRIBUTION N(μ, σ²):
- Symmetric bell curve around mean μ
- 68-95-99.7 rule: within 1σ, 2σ, 3σ of mean
- Standardize: Z = (X - μ) / σ
- P(a < X < b) → convert to Z-scores

GEOMETRIC DISTRIBUTION:
- P(first success on trial k) = (1-p)^(k-1) × p
- Mean: 1/p

HYPERGEOMETRIC:
- Sampling without replacement from finite population
- P(X=k) = C(K,k)×C(N-K,n-k) / C(N,n)
- N=population, K=successes in population, n=sample size

COMMON JEE PATTERNS:
- "At least one" → use complement: P(≥1) = 1 - P(0)
- "At most k" → sum P(0) + P(1) + ... + P(k)
- Binomial with large n, small p → approximate with Poisson (λ=np)

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Using binomial when sampling without replacement (use hypergeometric)
- Forgetting to check np and n(1-p) for normal approximation
- Wrong complement for "at least" problems
