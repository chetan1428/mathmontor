TOPIC: Quadratic Equations & Polynomials

QUADRATIC FORMULA:
For ax² + bx + c = 0:
x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / (2a)

DISCRIMINANT (D = b² - 4ac):
- D > 0: Two distinct real roots
- D = 0: One repeated real root (x = -b/2a)
- D < 0: Two complex conjugate roots (no real solution)

COMPLETING THE SQUARE:
ax² + bx + c = a(x + b/2a)² - (b²-4ac)/4a

VIETA'S FORMULAS (for roots α, β):
- α + β = -b/a
- α × β = c/a

FACTORING TIPS:
- Look for common factor first
- Sum of roots × product of roots approach
- For x² + bx + c: find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Forgetting ± in quadratic formula
- Sign errors when b is negative
- Dividing by 2a not just 2

SOLUTION TEMPLATE:
1. Write in standard form ax² + bx + c = 0
2. Identify a, b, c
3. Compute D = b² - 4ac
4. Apply formula or factor
5. Verify by substituting roots back

EXAMPLE: Solve 2x² - 5x + 3 = 0
a=2, b=-5, c=3
D = 25 - 24 = 1
x = (5 ± 1) / 4
x = 1.5 or x = 1
